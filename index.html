5 MOST
HAUNTED
PLACES IN ATHENS

Ohio University, located in Athens, OH, is considered to be the most haunted campus in America, if not the world. From a haunted asylum to a pentagram created by cemeteries, the city of Athens has a lot of spooky stories to tell. Here we go over the five most haunted locations in Athens.

1

The Ridges

The Ridges at Ohio University were a mental asylum until 1993. Many of the patients who died in this hospital still haunt the property, especially Margaret Schilling. Margaret wandered from her room one night and got locked in the boiler room, where she starved to death and the sun and heat then melted her corpse into the floor. An outline of her body remains to this day.

2

    Wilson Hall
    Two students have died on separate occasions in the mysterious Wilson Hall room 428. Before it was sealed off, people reported seeing items fly across the room. It also falls in the center of a pentagram created by connecting five Athens cemeteries.

3

    Angel Statue
    The West State Street Cemetery angel statue commemorates our fallen soldiers. While it doesn’t haunt those who enter the cemetery, it can sometimes be seen weeping, a frightening sight for any who witness it.

4

    Washington Hall
    A high school girls basketball team stayed in this dorm while visiting the university. On their way home, their bus crashed, killing the entire team. They are said to still roam the halls, dribbling their basketballs.

5

Crawford Hall
A student named Laura tragically fell to her death from the fourth floor of this building. Since her death, residents have reported seeing her in the hallways and have been unable to play the Bob Marley song, “Laura.”
